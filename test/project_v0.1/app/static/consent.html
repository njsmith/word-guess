UNIVERSITY OF CALIFORNIA - SAN DIEGO

CONSENT TO ACT AS A RESEARCH SUBJECT

Professor Roger Levy is conducting research to find out more about how people process written and spoken language, as well as other forms of auditory and visual information. There will be approximately 100 participants in the study.

If you agree to be in this study, the following may happen. You may be asked to fill out a short background questionnaire, which will take a few minutes. You may then be presented with one or more written or spoken words or sentences, images, and/or sounds. You may be asked to press buttons in response to these stimuli in order to indicate comprehension, to provide ratings or other judgments, or to answer questions regarding what you see and/or hear. All stimuli and responses will be of a generally inoffensive nature. If you have any questions before consenting, please contact Professor Roger Levy at rlevy@ucsd.edu or Nathaniel Smith at nathaniel.smith@ed.ac.uk.

There is no direct benefit to you from these procedures. The investigator, however, may learn more about how people process information. Participation in this study may involve some added risks or discomforts. There is also a slight risk of boredom, although many participants find participation in this study interesting. Your data will remain anonymous.

Participation in this study should take no longer than approximately 30 minutes. In consideration of your time you will be compensated monetarily for satisfactory completion of the study, as detailed in the description of this task on the Mechanical Turk website.

By consenting, you acknowledge that Professor Roger Levy or Nathaniel Smith has explained this study to you and answered any questions you have. If you have other questions or research-related problems, you may contact Professor Levy at 858-534-7219/rlevy@ucsd.edu. You may call the UC San Diego Human Research Protections Program at (858) 657-5100 for more information about this, to inquire about your rights as a research subject, or to report research-related problems.

Participation in this research is entirely voluntary. You may refuse to participate or withdraw at any time without jeopardy.

Research records will be kept confidential to the extent provided by law.

You have received a copy of this consent document to keep.

You agree to participate.
